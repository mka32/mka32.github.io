India's International Policies

India's foreign policy is shaped by a series of strategic and economic objectives that aim at ensuring national security and fostering economic growth.

Key International Policies

  • Non-Alignment Movement (NAM): India has remained committed to non-alignment in global politics, focusing on maintaining an independent foreign policy.
  • Neighbourhood First Policy: India's foreign policy emphasizes improving relations with its neighboring countries, including Pakistan, China, and the countries of South Asia.

  • Economic Diplomacy: India emphasizes trade relations and foreign investments to boost its economic growth, actively participating in global forums like the World Trade Organization (WTO) and G20.
