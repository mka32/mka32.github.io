India's Engagement with International Organizations

India is an active participant in various international organizations, focusing on both regional and global cooperation in diverse areas such as security, trade, climate change, and peacekeeping.

Key International Organizations India Participates In

  • United Nations (UN): India is a strong advocate for peace and security and has served as a non-permanent member of the UN Security Council multiple times. India continues to push for reforms, including permanent membership on the Council.
  • World Trade Organization (WTO): India plays an active role in shaping global trade policies and has been a key advocate for the interests of developing countries in the WTO.
  • BRICS: India is part of the BRICS grouping (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa), which focuses on promoting economic cooperation among emerging economies.
  • ASEAN: India has forged strong relationships with the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN), focusing on trade, security, and cultural exchange.
  • Commonwealth of Nations: India is a prominent member of the Commonwealth, participating in discussions on democracy, development, and human rights.
